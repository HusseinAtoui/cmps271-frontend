            The Maker’s Journey

            Beneath the sky so vast and wide,
            Where restless hearts and dreams collide,
            A maker walks, a mind untamed,
            Through shifting paths of fire and flame.

            With ink-stained hands and weary eyes,
            They shape the stars, they bend the skies,
            In pixels bright or strokes of light,
            A world is born, both dark and bright.

            They carve their thoughts in fleeting code,
            A tangled path, a winding road,
            Where logic meets the artist’s hand,
            A universe at their command.

            In midnight hush and candle’s glow,
            Where whispered thoughts begin to grow,
            They chase a dream not yet made whole,
            A spark that flickers in the soul.

            Each error calls, each flaw reveals
            The path to learn, the wound that heals.
            For every crack, for every seam,
            Is but a lesson wrapped in dream.

            And though the weight of doubt is strong,
            They find their voice, they write their song.
            A melody of ones and twos,
            A symphony of reds and blues.

            The critics speak, the echoes sting,
            Yet still, they rise, they build, they sing.
            For in the forge where minds ignite,
            The darkness bends before the light.

            So let them toil, let fingers bleed,
            For art is pain, and pain is need.
            To make, to mend, to break, to bind—
            A rebel’s heart, a maker’s mind.

            And when at last the work is done,
            The dawn will rise, the race be won.
            But still, they’ll wake to dream anew—
            For makers make, as makers do.

Thots Kweschens Queeries
